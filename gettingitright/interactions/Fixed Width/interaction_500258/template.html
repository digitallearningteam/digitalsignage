Guidance, supporting me to make the right choices

Values, guidance and boundaries. Making clear to the child / young person what is expected and why? Are household roles and rules of behaviour appropriate to the age and understanding of the child / young person? Are sanctions constructive and consistent? Are responses to behaviour appropriate, modelling behaviour that represents autonomous, responsible adult expectations? Is the child / young person treated with consideration and respect and encouraged to take responsibility within a safe and protective environment?

Knowing what is going to happen and when

Is the child / young person's life stable and predictable? Are routines and expectations appropriate and helpful to age and stage of development? Are the child's / young person's needs given priority within an environment that expects mutual consideration? Who are the family members and others important to them? Cna the people who look after them be relied on to be open and honest about family and household relationships, wider influences, needs, decisions and to involve them in matters which affect them? Transition issues must be fully explored for the child / young person during times of change.

Understanding my family's history, background and beliefs

Family and cultural history and issues of spiritulaity and faith. Does the child / young person have a good understanding of their own background, their family, extended relationships and their origins? Is their cultural hertiage given due prominence? Do those around the child / young person respect and value diversity?

Everyday care and help

This includes day-to-day physical and emotional care, food, clothing and housing. Enabling healthcare and educational opportunities. Meeting the child's / young person's needs over time, encouraging growth of responsibility and independence.

Keeping me safe

This includes day-to-day physical and emotional care, food, clothing and housing. Enabling healthcare and educational opportunities. Meeting the child's / young person's changing needs over time, encouraging growth of responsibility and independence.

Being there for me

Love, emotional warmth, attentiveness and engagement. Who are the people who can be relied on to recognise and respond to the child's / person's emotional needs? Who are the people with who the child has a particular bond? Who is of particular significance? Who does the child trust? Is there sufficient emotional security and responsiveness in the child's / young person's current caring environment?

Play, encouragement and fun

Stimulation and encouragement to learn and enjoy life. Who spends time with the child / young person, communicating, interacting and responding to the child's / young person's curiosity, providing an educationally rich environment? Is the child's / young person's progress enouraged by sensitive responses to interests and achievements and involvement in school activities. Is there someone to act as the child's / young person's mentor and champion?
